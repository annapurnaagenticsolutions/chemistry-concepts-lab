# Chemistry Concepts Lab — Subject Evaluation and Factual QA

## Scope reviewed

- 40 lessons across Classes 1–10.
- Materials, water, air, states of matter, separation, rusting, changes, safety, particles, acids/bases, reactions, combustion, atoms, mole concept, pH, metals, and carbon compounds.

## Evaluation

- **Factuality:** Good. The lab correctly frames Chemistry through materials, particles, changes, safety, reactions, and formulas.
- **Age fit:** Strong. Early classes stay observational; later classes introduce particle model, atoms, equations, and mole concept.
- **Safety:** Stronger than the other science labs because many lessons include explicit safety notes.
- **Main issue found:** Some simplified phrases were directionally correct but too loose if read literally.

## Applied fixes

1. Clarified steam/water vapour: invisible water vapour versus visible droplets formed by condensation.
2. Replaced “cooling removes heat” with heat-transfer wording.
3. Replaced “particles fixed in place” with “particles vibrate about fixed positions.”
4. Tightened compound/corrosion caveats.
5. Added factual QA banner and docs.

## Verdict

Chemistry is deployable after this patch. It should be presented as a concept lab with strong safety framing, not as a replacement for official textbooks or lab manuals.
